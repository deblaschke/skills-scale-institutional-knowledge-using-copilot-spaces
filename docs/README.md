# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

## Overview

OctoAcme follows a structured, iterative project management approach grounded in customer-first principles and clear ownership. The organization runs projects through five lifecycle phases: **Initiation** (defining the problem and stakeholders), **Planning** (breaking work into actionable increments), **Execution** (building and testing with regular team rhythm), **Release** (deploying to production with controlled risk), and **Close & Retrospective** (capturing learnings). The framework emphasizes delivering in small, testable increments while maintaining data-informed decisions and psychological safety.

OctoAcme defines three core personas with distinct responsibilities: **Project Managers** coordinate delivery, schedules, risks, and communications to ensure on-time, in-scope delivery; **Product Managers** define what should be built, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes to maximize customer value; and **Developers** implement features, write tests, and collaborate on design and technical risk mitigation. A structured communication cadence—weekly syncs between PM and Product Manager, twice-weekly team standups, and monthly stakeholder updates—keeps all parties aligned.

Quality assurance and execution are embedded throughout the development cycle. Teams use project boards with standardized workflows, maintain code quality through small pull requests and automated CI testing, and conduct regular demos and reviews. Daily standups focus on progress and blockers, while weekly delivery syncs review metrics against success criteria. The organization systematically identifies and monitors risks using a Risk Register, updated weekly and reviewed at leadership syncs.

## Key Principles

- **Customer-first**: Prioritize customer value and usability
- **Iterative delivery**: Deliver small, testable increments
- **Clear ownership**: Each project has named Project Manager and Product Lead
- **Data-informed decisions**: Measure impact and iterate based on evidence
- **Psychological safety**: Encourage feedback and learning
